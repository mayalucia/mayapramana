* The Compressed Path

You need three facts to understand the Bloch sphere. Here they are.

** Fact 1: Three real numbers

A spin-½ density matrix $\rho$ is 2×2, Hermitian, unit trace,
positive semi-definite. Hermiticity + unit trace leaves 3 free real
parameters. Therefore the state space is a subset of $\mathbb{R}^3$.

** Fact 2: The Pauli expansion

The Pauli matrices $\boldsymbol{\sigma} = (\sigma_x, \sigma_y, \sigma_z)$
are a basis for traceless Hermitian 2×2 matrices. Any such $\rho$
decomposes as:

$$\rho = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{I} + \mathbf{M} \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma})$$

with $\mathbf{M} = (M_x, M_y, M_z) = (\mathrm{Tr}(\rho\sigma_x),\, \mathrm{Tr}(\rho\sigma_y),\, \mathrm{Tr}(\rho\sigma_z))$.

** Fact 3: The sphere constraint

Positive semi-definiteness of $\rho$ requires $|\mathbf{M}| \leq 1$.

- $|\mathbf{M}| = 1$: pure state (surface). The spin is in a
  definite quantum state.
- $|\mathbf{M}| < 1$: mixed state (interior). The spin is entangled
  with its environment.
- $|\mathbf{M}| = 0$: maximally mixed (centre). Complete decoherence.

** What you need for the Bloch equations

The Bloch equations $d\mathbf{M}/dt = \gamma\mathbf{M}\times\mathbf{B} - \text{relaxation}$
are equations of motion for this vector.

- Precession ($\gamma\mathbf{M}\times\mathbf{B}$) rotates $\mathbf{M}$
  on a sphere of constant radius — preserves purity.
- $T_2$ decay shrinks the transverse components — moves the state
  inward.
- $T_1$ decay drives $M_z$ toward equilibrium — moves the state
  toward a target point on the $z$-axis.

That is all you need to follow the rest of this lesson. The derivation
from the von Neumann equation with Lindblad dissipation is in Lesson 02.
For the full parameter-counting argument, see the Landau expansion.
For a geometric picture of precession and relaxation as spirals on the
sphere, see the Thorne expansion.
